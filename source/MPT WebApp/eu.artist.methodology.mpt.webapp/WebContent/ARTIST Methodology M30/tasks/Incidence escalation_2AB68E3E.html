Task: IMRP.A6
The escalation of the incidence could be of two types:
- Functional escalation the incidence will need deeper technical knowledge, the incidence must be immediately escalated to the appropriate level support group
- Hierarchic escalation: If incidence is of a serious nature (for example urgent and with great impact) the appropriate Technical manager must be notified, for informational purposes at least. Hierarchic escalation is also used if the activities Investigation and Diagnosis and Resolution and Recovery are taking too long or proving too difficult.

      Functional escalation
      If the incidence is dealt as an incidence this implies that cannot be solved by the Helpdesk team. If it is obvious that the incidence will need deeper technical knowledge, the incidence must be immediately escalated to the appropriate level support group
      Hierarchic escalation
      If incidents are of a serious nature (for example Priority 1 incidents) the appropriate Tecnical manager must be notified, for informational purposes at least. Hierarchic escalation is also used if the ‘Investigation and Diagnosis’ and ‘Resolution and Recovery’ tasks are taking too long or proving too difficult.
